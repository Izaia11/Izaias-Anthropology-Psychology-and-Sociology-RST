Kanye West RST

By Izaia Freyer

Anthropology Psychology Sociology Citations

Anthropology

Cultural Practices: Kanye West is embedded in African American culture, blending secular and sacred experiences. His music reflects soul and gospel influences. His fashion brand, Yeezy, contributes to Black cultural visibility. His evangelical Christianity later in life intersected with public performance.

Kinship: His mother, Donda West, a professor, deeply influenced him. His father, Ray West, was a former Black Panther. His marriage to Kim Kardashian introduced celebrity culture into his family system. Kanye also refers to collaborators and fans as symbolic kin.

Sociolinguistics: Kanye uses AAVE and code-switches between elite speech and street vernacular. His lyrics blend metaphor, religion, and social critique. His public speech is often seen as erratic, interpreted as both mental health issues and defiance of public norms.

Summary: Kanye reflects and shapes culture through music, family, language, and belief systems.

Psychology

Kanye West publicly disclosed having bipolar disorder. His personality includes high creativity, narcissism, and emotional depth. Traumatic events like his mother’s death and a car accident impacted him.

Key Events: 2007 - mother’s death; 2016 - psychiatric hospitalization. Post-2016: erratic behaviour, public outbursts, controversial actions.

Internal Influences: Bipolar diagnosis, grief, openness, impulsivity.

External Influences: Fame, media, high-profile relationships, social media, cultural pressures.

Before vs After 2016: Pre-2016: Controlled ego, powerful music, public respect. Post-2016: Unpredictability, offensive statements, isolation.

Psychological Theory: Jung’s Analytic Psychology: Kanye’s shadow, persona vs self, and individuation failure describe his psychological tension.

Possible DSM-5-TR Diagnoses: Bipolar I, Narcissistic Personality Disorder, PTSD.

Sociology

Agents of Socialization: Family (Donda West), peers in hip-hop, education, religion, and media shaped Kanye’s identity.

Norms: Kanye challenges norms in music and fashion.

Roles: He plays the roles of artist, designer, entrepreneur, father, and religious leader.

Institutions: Music industry, fashion, media, religion.

Values: Individualism, creativity, free speech, and religious faith.
