Psychology, Sociology, and Anthropology RST

By Izaia Freyer | Focus: Kanye West

Anthropology: Cultural Influences on Kanye West

Cultural Practices: Kanye West is embedded in African American culture, particularly in hip-hop and gospel. His work blends secular and sacred traditions. Early albums sample soul music; "Jesus is King" reflects gospel influence. His Yeezy brand redefines Black aesthetics in fashion.

Spiritual Beliefs: In later years, he leaned heavily into evangelical Christianity, intersecting faith with performance art and public identity.

Kinship: Kanye was raised by his mother, Donda West, who was a professor and his greatest influence. His father, Ray West, exposed him to activism and journalism. His marriage to Kim Kardashian added a celebrity kinship network. He refers to close collaborators and fans as extended family.

Sociolinguistics: Kanye code-switches between AAVE and formal English, reflecting cultural duality. His lyrics include metaphor, slang, and social critique. His erratic interviews challenge public norms and spotlight issues of race and mental health.

Psychology: Kanye West's Internal and External Influences

Public Persona: Creative, controversial, and open about his bipolar diagnosis, which he calls a "superpower."

Key Life-Altering Events:

  • 2007: Death of Donda West
  • 2016: Psychiatric emergency
  • Post-2016: Erratic behavior, public controversies, and presidential run

Internal Influences: Bipolar disorder, high openness, trauma from his mother's death, and personality traits like narcissism and impulsivity.

External Influences: Media scrutiny, fame, celebrity relationships, and cultural expectations around Black masculinity.

Before vs. After Mental Breakdown

Before (Pre-2016): Controlled confidence, groundbreaking albums, emotionally deep lyrics, artistic focus.

After (Post-2016): Erratic speech, delusions, isolation, controversial behavior, antisemitic remarks, and presidential ambitions.

Psychological Theories

Jung’s Analytic Psychology: Kanye displays the shadow archetype through his projections and public outbursts. His conflict between public persona ("Ye") and true self suggests individuation failure. Ego inflation and refusal to confront inner trauma contribute to instability.

Possible Diagnoses (DSM-5-TR):

  • Bipolar I Disorder: Diagnosed, with manic episodes and possible depression
  • Narcissistic Personality Disorder: Publicly visible symptoms (lack of empathy, self-grandiosity)
  • PTSD: Possible, due to traumatic life events (accident, grief, divorce)

Sociology: Kanye West's Social Shaping

Agents of Socialization: Influenced heavily by Donda West, peers in Chicago’s music scene, media, education (college dropout), and faith-based gatherings.

Key Concepts:

Norms: Kanye challenges social norms in music, fashion, and public discourse.

Roles: Musician, entrepreneur, father, fashion designer, religious leader.

Institutions: Connected to music, fashion, media, religion.

Values: Advocates for creativity, faith, free speech, and individualism.
